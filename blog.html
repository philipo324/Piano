July, 10, 2015

Today, we are reviewing The Marriage of Figaro-Overture by Mozart:





This overture was the first of three composed/collaborated by Mozart. The piece includes blaring trumpets and gives the great impression for happiness. The entire piece is filled with excitement. The orchestra of this certain piece played everything perfectly from the dynamics to getting the perfect sound. Overall, this piece was beautiful and beautifully played from the orchestra. An interesting fact, this piece was originally containing a melancholy solo for the middle section, for contrast. However Mozart decided not to because of the tone of this piece.




July, 3, 2015

Today, we are reviewing Fur Elise by Beethoven:





This iconic piece is performed beautifully with everything done right. The piece starts off with the a section with the classic two note melody. The piece must be played with a quiet hand, watching out for any indication in the piece, which is exactly what the pianist has done. The piece then transfers into the B section which takes a dramatic turn into a much happier piece with many staccatos. While at the same time it changes moods entirely. Then the piece goes into the C section which begins with darker chords and continues on into the beautiful a minor arpeggio, going into a descending harmonic scale. Which ends up with the original melodic A section. Overall, everything is done perfectly from the notes to the dynamics and gives the impression that the pianist know what he is doing.
